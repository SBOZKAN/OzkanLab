DynamicsDB: Enzyme Dynamics Database

The Enzyme Dynamics Database (DynamicsDB) is the first large-scale database of protein dynamics. It provides an extensive collection of enzyme dynamics signatures associated with known functions. This database categorizes information into clusters spanning enzyme functional classes, deepening our understanding of the relationship between protein dynamics and function.

DynamicsDB employs the Dynamic Flexibility Index (DFI) to analyze a diverse array of enzymes with significant functional roles. By consolidating dynamic and evolutionary signatures into a unified database, DynamicsDB supports focused studies on the complex interplay between protein sequence, structure, dynamics, and function.

Innovative Aspects of DynamicsDB

  • First large-scale database of protein dynamics, linking dynamics to function for enzymes.
  • Develops new protein design methods that consider protein dynamics during the design phase.
  • Utilizes novel computational techniques like the Dynamic Flexibility Index (DFI) for analyzing protein flexibility profiles.

Introduction to DynamicsDB

Proteins are essential biomolecules that conduct diverse functions necessary for life. Despite their complexity, proteins are built from long chains of twenty simple amino acids. Understanding the relationship between amino acid sequences and protein functions has significant applications in disease treatment, green chemistry, and new energy sources.

The Enzyme Dynamics Database aims to unravel the intricate connection between protein dynamics and function. By using computational methods such as the Dynamic Flexibility Index (DFI) and integrating structural and evolutionary analyses, DynamicsDB offers insights into enzyme dynamics that can lead to innovations in protein design and biomedicine.

Features of DynamicsDB

  • Comprehensive database of enzyme dynamics signatures.
  • Clustering of enzymes by structure, dynamics, evolution.
  • DFI-based analysis for detailed flexibility profiling.
  • Tools for visualizing and comparing protein dynamics.

Use Cases

DynamicsDB can be utilized in various research scenarios, including:

  • Investigating the relationship between protein dynamics and enzymatic function.
  • Designing new enzymes with desired dynamic properties for industrial applications.
  • Studying the evolutionary conservation and dynamic features in proteins together.
  • Developing new therapeutic proteins with optimized dynamics for better efficacy.
